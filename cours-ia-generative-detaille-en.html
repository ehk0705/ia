Detailed Course: Generative AI

Understand how generative models learn to produce text, images, audio or code ‚Äì and what their strengths and limitations are.

1. Core principles

  • Difference between predictive AI and generative AI
  • Probabilistic models: learning a data distribution
  • Log-likelihood, sampling, temperature

2. Main families of generative models

  • Variational autoencoders (VAE)
  • GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks)
  • Diffusion models
  • Large language models (LLMs)

3. Large language models (LLMs)

  • Transformers, attention, embeddings
  • Pretraining on large corpora
  • Fine-tuning and alignment (RLHF, etc.)
  • Applications: chatbots, agents, assistants, code generation

4. Examples of applications

  • Text generation (summaries, e-mails, documentation)
  • Image generation (art, concept design, prototyping)
  • Code completion and developer copilots
  • Data synthesis for training

5. Limitations and risks

  • Hallucinations: plausible but incorrect answers
  • Bias and stereotypes in training data
  • Ethics, copyright and safety issues
